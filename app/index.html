Mission: To create an enabling environment that will empower the girl-child (student) to be excellent academically, upright morally and to render good service to humanity.

Vision: To train and produce diligent girls of excellent character who should attain as high an academic standard as they possibly can.

Here’s how we got started…

The Accra Wesley High School was established in 2003 by the Methodist Church Ghana at the premises of the Rev. Joseph Thomas Clegg Memorial Methodist Church, Kaneshie Accra. The establishment of the school seeks to provide the girl-child education in another Girls school which is envisaged to be comparable with other good girls schools if not the best in the not-too- distant future.

The school’s population now stands at about 1600 with 96 members of staff and housed a two-storey building inaugurated in March, 2012 by the president of the Methodist Church Ghana Most Rev. Prof. Emmanuel K. Asante. The school currently runs four (5) programs namely, General Arts, Business Accounting, Home Economics, General Science and Visual Art.

In March, 2016, Very Rev(Mrs.) Jane D. Dor who took over from Mrs. Emelia Addy as the substantive Headmistress on attaining the compulsory retirement, Very Rev (Mrs.) Jane D. Dor retired from the Ghana Education Service (GES) and handed over to Mrs. Cynthia Essibrey-Annan (the then Assistant Headmistress, Academic Affairs) who also went on retirement this year. Currently Miss. Letitia Bray is the substantive Headmistress.
